Frequently Asked Questions

How do I let my student know their schedule?

Before each student’s rotation, you will need to click on the “Email schedule” button. This can be found under the Students tab.

Where do my students report for clinic? Are there any lectures?

Start times by clinic site:

Clinic Site Floor Start Time
The Emory Clinic (TEC)
1525 Clifton Road		 3rd Floor 8:20am* & 1:00pm
*Except Thursday mornings start at 10am
Grady Clinic 1st Floor (A/B Tower) 8:15am & 1:15pm
VA Clinic 1st Floor (medicine subspecialties clinic area) 8:30am* & 1:30pm
*Except Tuesday mornings start at 9:30am

We also welcome rotators to attend Emory Dermatology Grand Rounds, which are held on the 1st, 3rd, and 5th Thursday of the month. It is in the Brown Auditorium at Emory Clinic Building A (1365 Clifton Road) from 7:45am to 10am.

What if there is not an open or available clinic during a time that I want to register a student?

Unfortunately, due to high volume and pre-existing agreements, we are not able to accommodate all requests. Please check back in the future to see if any pots become available.

What if I registered a student for a rotation, but he/she can no longer attend?

You can do this through the same calendar that you used to initially register the student. Please see “New user instructions” document for details. If a student has to cancel a rotation, please do so on the calendar as soon as possible so that that spot may be available to other students who are interested.

I received an email that a clinic that I registered a student for has been canceled. What does this mean?

Unfortunately, the clinic you registered for is no longer in session that day. Please go back to the main calendar you used to register the student to see if there are any alternative options available

I still have questions. Is there someone I can call?

Please call our dermatology rotation coordinator at 404-727-3669 with any questions. Please leave a detailed message with how we can reach you.

The Login Screen

Currently registered users

  • If you know your credentials, enter your email and password into the appropriate boxes and click the “Login” button

  • If you have forgotten your password, click the “Forgot your password?” link towards the bottom of the page

    1. On the following page, enter your e-mail address into the box provided and click the “Submit” button

    2. An email will be sent to your email address containing a link

    3. Click this link and you will be directed to a page where you will be prompted to reset your password

    4. Once you’ve reset your password and clicked “Submit”, you will be logged in to the system

    • Not currently registered users

  • Request Access through the link, “Don’t have an account? Sign up”, at the bottom of the page

    1. On the following page, enter your first name, last name and your e-mail address in the appropriate boxes and select your department from the drop down list. Then, click the “Register” button to submit your information as a request for credentials to be able to login to and use the application.

    2. Once you submit your request, an administrator will have to approve your credentials

    3. If and when you are approved by the administrator, you will receive an e-mail with a link to a screen where you can create your password

    4. Once you enter your password into the correct field and confirm it, click the “Submit” button and you will automatically be logged into the system

    • Administrator Functions

    Log out

  • To logout of the application, click the red “Log Out” button located on the far right of the navigation bar

    • View pending and current coordinators

  • Pending Coordinators

    1. A list of all Coordinators pending approval appears under the title, “Coordinators Pending Approval” at the top of the “External Residency Coordinators” page that you are directed to after logging in and after clicking the “Coordinators” link in the navigation bar. Each pending coordinator should have a name, e-mail address, phone number and department listed.

    2. To approve a Pending Coordinator, click the blue “Approve” button to the right of the coordinator’s name

    3. Once you approve a Pending Coordinator, this button will change to "Re-Approve" until they have finished the sign up process. If a coordinator needs you to re-send them the credentials to finish the sign up process, come back to this page and click the "Re-Approve" button.

    4. To deny a Pending Coordinator, click the red “Deny” button to the right of the coordinator’s name and below the “Approve” button

  • Current Coordinators

    1. A list of all current coordinators appears under the title, “Current Coordinators” below the “Coordinators Pending Approval” on the page that you are directed to after logging in and after clicking the “Coordinators” link in the navigation bar. Each coordinator’s name should be displayed in an individual box.

    2. To view each coordinator’s information, click on the coordinator’s name(the text should change color when your mouse is hovering over the text) and the coordinator’s individual box should expand. Once their individual box is expanded, you should be able to see the coordinator’s name, e-mail address, phone number and department. To collapse this box, click the coordinator’s name at the top again.

    3. If a current coordinator has requested you reset their password

      1. Click their name to expand their individual box if it is not already open

      2. In the top right corner, click the button that says “Reset Password”

      3. The coordinator will then be sent an email containing a link that they need to follow to be able to reset the password themselves

    • View all students

  • Navigate to the Students page by clicking the “Students” Link in the navigation bar.

  • Under the title, “All Students”, each student’s name should appear in their own individual box

  • To view more information about a particular student:

    1. Click on the student’s name(the text should change color when your mouse is hovering over the text) and the student’s individual box should expand.

    2. Once their individual box is expanded, you should be able to see the student’s name, e-mail address, the type of student they are, the e-mail address of the coordinator in charge of this particular student, and a list of all the events they are currently signed up for.

    3. To collapse this box, click the student’s name at the top again.

    • Email a student their schedule

  • Navigate to the Students page by clicking the “Students” Link in the navigation bar.

  • Click on a student’s name to expand their individual box.

  • Once the box has been expanded, you should see a blue button in the upper right corner of this box that says, “E-mail Schedule to Student”. Click this button.

  • A modal will pop up confirming that the email was sent. The email will contain their schedule and any necessary supplemental information.

    • Email all students their schedules

  • Navigate to the Students page by clicking the “Students” link in the navigation bar.

  • Towards the bottom left of the page, there is a blue button labelled “E-mail All Students”. Click this button.

  • A modal will appear asking you to confirm that you want to continue with this action. If you wish to cancel, click the blue "Cancel" button at the bottom right of the modal.

  • If you wish to continue, click the blue "Confirm" button in the bottom right of the modal and an email will be sent to all students containing their schedule and any necessary supplemental information.

    • Export all students and their information to a CSV file and then delete them

  • Navigate to the Students page by clicking the “Students” link in the navigation bar.

  • Towards the bottom left of the page, there is a blue button labelled “Export and Delete Students”. Click this button.

  • A modal will appear to make sure you want to complete this action. If you do not, click the blue "Cancel" button in the bottom right of the modal.

  • If you do wish to continue, click the blue "Confirm" button at the bottom right of the modal and all of the students and their information will be exported to a CSV file for you to download, and then all of the students who are not signed up for a future event will be deleted.

    • Delete one particular student

  • Navigate to the Students page by clicking the “Students” Link in the navigation bar.

  • Under the title, “All Students”, each student’s name should appear in their own individual box.

  • Find the student that you want to delete and click their name to expand their personal information box.

  • In their personal information box towards the upper right, there is a red button labelled “Delete Student”. Click this button.

  • A modal will appear asking you to confirm that you want to delete this particular student. If you do not with to continue, click the blue "Cancel" button in the bottom right of the modal.

  • If you wish to continue deleting the student, click the blue "Confirm" button in the bottom right of the modal and the student will be deleted.

    • View the calendar of events

  • Click the “Schedule” link in the navigation bar.

  • The page you are directed to will display a calendar in a month-view format.

  • If a day contains any events, a blue circle will be present in the top left corner of the day and contain a number for how many events are on that day.

    • Add a new event to the calendar

  • If not already on the Schedule page, click the “Schedule” link in the navigation bar.

  • In the top right-hand corner above the calendar, click the yellow “New Half-Day Session” button.

  • A “Create Event” modal should pop up. On this modal:

    1. Choose the location of the event from the dropdown menu under “Location”

    2. Enter the name of the physician on duty for this event in the text box under “Physician on Duty”

    3. Select all of the learner types who are eligible to sign up for this event by checking the box next to each eligible learner type under “Learner Types”

      • You can add additional learner types by clicking "Add Other Learner Type."

      • Learner types are case-sensitive and cannot include commas.

      • Selecting "Allow Any Learner Type" will allow any learner type to sign up for this event, not just the learner types present in the current list.

    4. Choose the date for this event by clicking on the magnifying glass below “Date” and clicking the date you want from the calendar modal that appears once the magnifying glass is clicked on

    5. Choose the time of day for the event (AM or PM) by clicking the radio button for the corresponding time of day under “Time”

    6. Choose to repeat or not repeat the event by choosing either the “None”, “Weekly”, or "Monthly" radio button under “Repeat”

      • If you chose to repeat "Weekly", you must pick the exact end date for the repeating event.

      • If you chose to repeat "Monthly", you must pick the end month for the repeating event. Indicate your choice by selecting the final day of that month.

    7. Choose whether or not to prevent the session from thawing. By default, all sessions will thaw two weeks before their start date.

  • Once you have entered all of the event’s information, press the “Submit” button in the bottom right corner of the modal.

  • If you wish to cancel creating the event at any time, click the Cancel button in the bottom right corner of the modal.

    • Edit an event

  • If not already on the Schedule page, click the “Schedule” link in the navigation bar.

  • Find the event in the calendar that you want to edit by navigating to your desired month and clicking on your desired day to view all the events for that day.

  • To the right of the event details for the event that you want to edit, there is a pencil icon. Click this icon and a modal will pop up that looks exactly like the “Create Half Day Session”. All of the current information for this event will be populated in the fields.

  • Change any information that you wish to change and click the blue “Save” button.

  • If you get to this point and realize you just want to delete the entire event all together, press the red “Delete” button in the bottom left corner of the modal.

  • If you get to this point and realize that you don’t want to make any changes, you can click the blue “Cancel” button in the bottom right corner of the modal.

    • Delete an event

  • If not already on the Schedule page, click the “Schedule” link in the navigation bar.

  • Find the event in the calendar that you want to delete by navigating to your desired month and clicking on your desired day to view all the events for that day.

  • To the right of the event details for the event that you want to delete, there is a trash can icon. Click this icon and the event should disappear from the list of the day’s events.

  • Once the event is deleted, an e-mail will be sent to anyone who may have signed up for the event notifying them that you have deleted/cancelled it

    • View your templates

  • Navigate to the “Manage Templates” page by clicking the “Manage Templates” link in the navigation bar.

  • On this page, under the title, “My Templates”, each template you have created should appear in their own individual box.

  • To view more information about a particular template, click on the template’s name (the text should change color when your mouse is hovering over the text) and the template’s individual box should expand. Once their individual box is expanded, you should be able to see the template’s learner type and a table that lists all of the sessions this template contains with each session’s location, physician on duty, the week, the day of the week, and the time.

  • To close this box, just click on the template’s name again.

    • Create a template

  • Navigate to the template page if not already there.

  • Below the list of current templates, there is a blue button labeled “Add Template”. Click this button.

  • Once clicked, a form will appear below this button that contains a text box labled "Title" to input the name of the template you wish to create and a drop down menu. Use the drop down menu to select the type of learner who will be allowed to sign up for sessions in this template. For example, if this is a "Sub-I" template, you would select "Sub-I Medical Student."

  • After filling in the template name and selecting a learner type, click the blue “Submit” button located just below the form.

  • Once you hit submit, your new template will be immediately added to the list above.

    • Add a session to a template

  • Navigate to the template page if not already there.

  • Open the individual box of the template you wish to add a session to by clicking on the template's name.

  • In the top right corner of the individual box, click the yellow “Add Session” button.

  • A “Create New Session” modal will appear with a drop down menu to choose the location of the session, text boxes to enter the physician on duty, the week of rotation, and the day of the week (which must be entered in number format), and radio buttons to choose whether this is an AM or PM session.

  • Once you have put in all the session's information, click the “Submit” button that appears in the bottom right corner of the modal. The screen will refresh. To see the newly created session, click on the template's name again. Notice that the session is now listed in the table labeled "Sessions."

  • If at any point, you change your mind about adding the session, click the “Cancel” button in the bottom right of the modal.

    • Edit a template

  • Navigate to the template page if not already there.

  • Open the individual box of the template you want to edit.

  • To edit the learner type of the template, click the pencil icon to the right of the heading labeled "Learner Type," which is located at the top of a template’s individual box. A modal will appear, allowing you to select a new learner type from a dropdown list.

  • To edit an individual session in a template, locate the session in the table and click the pencil icon. An "Edit Session" modal will appear prepopulated with the current session information. Change the desired information, then click save, or cancel if you do not wish to save your changes. If there is an error in the information you changed or you attempt to change a session so that it conflicts with another session already in the template, a red error message will display in the bottom right hand corner of the modal. You will not be able to save the session changes until all errors have been fixed.

  • If you have made any changes to a template that has already been added to the calendar, the changes you have made will not be made to the sessions currently in the calendar. Those changes would have to be made directly to the events in the calendar if you wish to make them.

    • Delete a template

  • Navigate to the template page if not already there.

  • Open the individual box of the template you want to delete.

  • Click the "Delete Template" button in the bottom left corner of the box.

  • Click the "Confirm" button in the confirmation modal to delete the entire template.

    • Add a template to the calendar

  • Navigate to the calendar page by clicking the “Schedule” link in the navigation bar.

  • At the top right above the calendar, click the blue “Add Template to Schedule” button.

  • A modal will appear with a drop down menu for you to choose from the templates you have created, a place to choose the start date to apply this template to, and whether or not you wish to prevent the sessions from thawing.

  • Once you are finished, hit the blue “Submit” button in the bottom right of the modal.

  • If at any point you wish to cancel, hit the blue “Cancel” button in the bottom right of the modal.

  • Adding a template to the calendar creates new half-day sessions inside the calendar. The new sessions inside the calendar will not be affected by any changes made from inside the "Manage Templates" view (or vice-versa).

    • Add a new student

  • Navigate to the Students page by clicking the “Students” link in the navigation bar.

  • At the bottom left of the page, find the blue button, “Add Students”, and click it.

  • A form will open up underneath the button.

  • Enter the student’s name, e-mail into the appropriate fields and select their type from the dropdown menu.

  • Click the blue “Submit” button at the bottom left of the form to submit the student.

    • View the calendar of events

  • Navigate to the calendar containing all the events, click the “Schedule” link in the navigation bar.

  • The page you are directed to will display a calendar in a month-view format.

  • If a day contains any events, a blue circle will be present in the top left corner of the day and contain a number for how many events are on that day.

    • View events on a particular day

  • Navigate to the calendar containing all the events, click the “Schedule” link in the navigation bar.

  • Navigate to your desired month (or week depending on which view you are currently in) using the “Next” and “Previous” buttons located above the calendar.

  • Find the day you are looking for and and click anywhere within that day’s box.

    1. If a day does not have any events scheduled and therefore no blue dot with a number inside of it appears within that day, the day will not be clickable

  • A panel will expand below the day with a list of all that day’s events and their details such as the location of the event, the doctor associated with the event and whether it is an AM or PM event.

    1. If the dot next to an event is yellow, the event is available for scheduling.

    2. If the dot next to an event is gray, the event is not available for scheduling.

  • To collapse this panel, just click anywhere within that day’s box again.

    • Sign a student up for an event

  • Navigate to the calendar containing all the events by clicking the “Schedule” link in the navigation bar.

  • In the top right above the calendar, there is a dropdown menu that says “Select Student”. Select the student who you want to sign up for an event from this dropdown menu.

  • Find the event in the calendar that you want to sign this student up for.

  • If the event has a yellow dot next to it, the event is open and the student you selected is eligible to sign up for it. If the dot is gray, then either the event has already been taken or your student is not eligible to sign up for it.

  • To sign your student up for this event if the dot is yellow, click the plus sign that appears to the right of the event details.

  • Note: It is possible for a yellow event to actually be taken (Suppose two coordinators open the app on their own computers simultaneously. If the first coordinator signs their learner up for an event, the second coordinator will not see this change in real-time). In this case, you will see an error notification saying that the event has already been taken, and the event will now be grayed out.

  • If the session you choose to sign up for is part of a group of sessions, a pop up will appear to notify you after you have registered your student for the session.

  • Any sessions that are gray because your student is not eligible for it will open up to all types of students two weeks before the event occurs if it has not already been filled, so be sure to check back within that time frame if your student needs more sessions

    • Unregister a student from an event

  • Navigate to the calendar containing all the events by clicking the “Schedule” link in the navigation bar.

  • In the top right above the calendar, there is a dropdown menu that says “Select Student”. Select the student who you want to unregister for an event from this dropdown menu.

  • Find the event in the calendar that you want to unregister this student from.

  • Next to this event there should be an “X” icon. Click this icon and your student should be unregistered for that event.

  • You should see the “X” icon change back to a “+” icon after you have clicked it. This confirms that you were successful in unregistering your student from the event.
